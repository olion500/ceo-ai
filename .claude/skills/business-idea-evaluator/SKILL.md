# Business Idea Evaluator

## Evaluation Framework

### Core Scoring Dimensions (1-10 scale)

**1. Problem Intensity (ç—› Tong - Pain)**
- How badly do people need this solved?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Nice-to-have, convenience feature
  - 4-6: Moderate pain, people seek solutions actively
  - 7-9: Severe pain, people pay premium to solve
  - 10: Critical business blocker, must-solve urgently

**2. Market Size (å¸‚ Shi - Market)**
- How many potential customers exist?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: <10K potential customers
  - 4-6: 10K-100K potential customers
  - 7-9: 100K-1M potential customers
  - 10: >1M potential customers

**3. Competition Level (ç«¶ Jing - Compete)**
- How difficult to compete? (Lower is better)
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Crowded market, many strong competitors
  - 4-6: Moderate competition, niche opportunities
  - 7-9: Limited competition, underserved market
  - 10: No direct competitors, blue ocean

**4. Monetization Clarity (é‡‘ Jin - Money)**
- How clear is the path to revenue?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Unclear how to charge, ad-dependent
  - 4-6: Proven model exists, pricing unclear
  - 7-9: Clear pricing, proven willingness to pay
  - 10: Pre-validated, customers asking to pay

**5. Differentiation (ç¨ Du - Unique)**
- How unique is your approach/solution?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Commodity, no clear differentiation
  - 4-6: Better UX/pricing than alternatives
  - 7-9: Unique approach or positioning
  - 10: Paradigm shift, completely new category

**6. Time to Market (é€Ÿ Su - Speed)**
- How quickly can you launch MVP? (Faster is better)
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: >6 months to viable product
  - 4-6: 3-6 months to MVP
  - 7-9: 1-3 months to MVP
  - 10: Can launch in <1 month

**7. Solo/Small Team Fit (ç¨ Du - Solo)**
- How suitable for 1-3 person team?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Requires large team, complex operations
  - 4-6: Doable but challenging alone
  - 7-9: Well-suited for small team
  - 10: Perfect for solo founder

**8. Skills Match (èƒ½ Neng - Ability)**
- How well do your skills match requirements?
- Score 1-10 where:
  - 1-3: Need to learn most required skills
  - 4-6: Have some skills, gaps exist
  - 7-9: Strong skill match, minor gaps
  - 10: Perfect match, existing expertise

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Problem Definition

```markdown
## Problem Statement

**What problem are you solving?**
[One sentence description]

**Who experiences this problem?**
- Primary audience: [Description]
- Secondary audience: [Description]
- Market segment: [B2B/B2C/Developer tools/etc]

**How are they solving it today?**
- Current solution 1: [Description]
- Current solution 2: [Description]
- Workarounds: [Description]

**Why are current solutions inadequate?**
- Pain point 1: [Description]
- Pain point 2: [Description]
- Pain point 3: [Description]
```

### Step 2: Evidence Collection

For each scoring dimension, collect evidence:

**Problem Intensity Evidence:**
- [ ] Found forum posts/tweets complaining about problem
- [ ] People currently paying for inadequate solutions
- [ ] Workarounds are time-consuming or expensive
- [ ] Problem directly impacts revenue/costs
- [ ] Asked 10+ potential users about pain level

**Market Size Evidence:**
- [ ] Estimated addressable market size
- [ ] Similar products have X users/customers
- [ ] Google Keyword Planner search volume
- [ ] Reddit/forum community size
- [ ] Industry reports or analyst estimates

**Competition Evidence:**
- [ ] Listed all direct competitors
- [ ] Analyzed competitor pricing
- [ ] Reviewed competitor reviews/complaints
- [ ] Identified market gaps
- [ ] Tested competitor products

**Monetization Evidence:**
- [ ] Competitors' pricing models researched
- [ ] Willingness-to-pay survey conducted
- [ ] Similar products' revenue multiples
- [ ] Customer budget information
- [ ] Talked to 5+ people about what they'd pay

**Differentiation Evidence:**
- [ ] Unique approach validated
- [ ] Tested messaging with target audience
- [ ] Identified positioning angle
- [ ] Competitive advantages listed
- [ ] Analyzed why people would switch

### Step 4: Overall Assessment

**Total Score: [X/80]**

**Interpretation:**
- 0-25: High risk, significant concerns, recommend pivoting
- 26-40: Moderate potential, needs major improvements
- 41-55: Good potential, some risks to address
- 56-70: Strong potential, favorable odds
- 71-80: Exceptional opportunity, proceed with confidence

**Risk Distribution:**
- Dimension scores <4: Critical risks requiring attention
- Dimension scores 4-6: Moderate risks to mitigate
- Dimension scores >6: Favorable factors to leverage

## Validation Checklist

Before finalizing evaluation:

**Customer Validation:**
- [ ] Talked to 10+ potential customers
- [ ] Confirmed problem severity (not assumed)
- [ ] Asked about current solutions and pain points
- [ ] Discussed pricing expectations
- [ ] Got commitments (email, LOI, or pre-order)

**Market Validation:**
- [ ] Researched market size with data
- [ ] Identified 3+ direct competitors
- [ ] Analyzed 5+ indirect competitors
- [ ] Read competitor reviews (50+ reviews)
- [ ] Monitored Reddit/forums for 2+ weeks

**Product Validation:**
- [ ] Created mockups or prototype
- [ ] Showed to potential customers
- [ ] Gathered feedback on approach
- [ ] Validated key features priority
- [ ] Confirmed MVP scope is achievable

**Business Model Validation:**
- [ ] Researched competitor pricing
- [ ] Calculated unit economics
- [ ] Estimated CAC for your channels
- [ ] Projected realistic LTV
- [ ] Checked LTV:CAC ratio feasibility

## Common Evaluation Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Overweighting Personal Passion
**Problem:** "I love this idea" bias clouds objective assessment
**Fix:** Separate personal interest from market validation
**Question:** Would this score well if someone else pitched it?

### Mistake 2: Ignoring Competition
**Problem:** "I haven't seen competitors" = "No market"
**Fix:** Research thoroughly, competition often indicates demand
**Question:** Why hasn't this been solved if it's a real problem?

### Mistake 3: Overestimating Market Size
**Problem:** Using TAM instead of realistic SAM/SOM
**Fix:** Calculate what YOU can actually reach in Year 1-2
**Question:** How many customers can I realistically acquire?

### Mistake 4: Underestimating Time to Market
**Problem:** "Just 3 months" turns into 9 months
**Fix:** Add 2-3x buffer for unknowns
**Question:** What could go wrong that I haven't anticipated?

### Mistake 5: Assuming "Build it and they will come"
**Problem:** Great product, no distribution plan
**Fix:** Evaluate distribution difficulty explicitly
**Question:** How will customer #1, #10, #100 find me?

**Idea:** Shopify app for automatic product description generation using AI

- Problem Intensity: 8/10 (Store owners hate writing descriptions)
- Market Size: 7/10 (2M+ Shopify stores)
- Competition: 6/10 (Few AI-focused competitors)
- Monetization: 9/10 (Clear $29/mo subscription model)
- Differentiation: 7/10 (AI angle + Shopify integration)
- Time to Market: 7/10 (Can build in 6-8 weeks)
- Solo/Team Fit: 8/10 (Perfect for solo dev)
- Skills Match: 8/10 (Have required skills)

**Why High Score:** Clear problem, proven market, fast to build, monetization obvious.

### Medium Score Example (45/80)

**Idea:** Social network for book lovers

- Problem Intensity: 5/10 (Nice-to-have, not urgent)
- Market Size: 8/10 (Millions of readers)
- Competition: 3/10 (Goodreads, LibraryThing, many others)
- Monetization: 4/10 (Ads or premium unclear)
- Differentiation: 5/10 (Better UX, but commoditized)
- Time to Market: 5/10 (3-4 months for MVP)
- Solo/Team Fit: 6/10 (Possible but challenging alone)
- Skills Match: 6/10 (Need to learn recommendation engines)

**Why Medium Score:** Large market but tough competition, unclear monetization, commoditized product.

### Low Score Example (28/80)

**Idea:** AI-powered meal planning with grocery delivery

- Problem Intensity: 6/10 (People want this)
- Market Size: 7/10 (Everyone eats)
- Competition: 2/10 (Many established players)
- Monetization: 5/10 (Subscription possible but low margin)
- Differentiation: 4/10 (AI isn't unique anymore)
- Time to Market: 2/10 (Complex integrations, 6+ months)
- Solo/Team Fit: 1/10 (Need team for operations)
- Skills Match: 4/10 (Need food/logistics expertise)

**Why Low Score:** Competitive market, complex to build, requires team, low differentiation.
