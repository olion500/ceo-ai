# Marc Chapon Dog Products Dropshipping Success Story


## The Story

### Problem Discovery

Marc Chapon studied **Hospitality/Restaurant management** in Paris and Switzerland with basic marketing knowledge but limited e-commerce experience. He identified dropshipping as an opportunity because:

- Wanted to **quit his full-time job** and become independent
- Had **genuine passion and knowledge** about dogs (personal interest)
- Saw opportunity in **underserved personalization market** (custom dog tags, bracelets)
- Validated **evergreen demand** through Google Trends (consistent year-round interest in dog products)

**Key insight:** Combined **personal passion (dogs) + data validation (Google Trends) + market gap (personalization)** instead of chasing trending products.

### MVP Development

**Timeline:** Estimated 2-4 weeks to reach break-even ($1,000 invested)

**Initial Features:**

- Shopify store with Brooklyn theme
- 25-50 curated products (NOT thousands)
- Focus on **personalized items** (custom dog tags, bracelets with pet names)
- Product collections organized by category
- Downloadable dog care guide (lead magnet)
- Sticky navigation for mobile browsing
- HD product images from suppliers

**Tech Stack:**

  - Initial: PayPal, 2Checkout
  - Later: Stripe (via international business registration)
  - Loox (photo reviews and social proof)
  - Recart (Facebook Messenger abandoned cart recovery)
  - TxtCart (SMS marketing for cart abandonment)
  - Shopney (mobile optimization)

**Development Process:**

- Rejected AliExpress due to "poor descriptions, generic quality, and uncertain shipping"
- Chose Spocket for **US and European suppliers** (better quality, faster shipping, higher margins)
- Limited to **25-50 curated products** instead of importing thousands
- Focused on **product customization** capability (personalized items)
- Created **custom logo in Canva** (yellow = happiness, brown = earthiness)

### Launch Strategy

**Platform:** Facebook & Instagram Ads (primary), Email Marketing (secondary)
**Date:** Not specified (estimated 2018-2019)

**Pre-launch:**

- Researched competitor stores and customer reviews on Amazon
- Analyzed Google Trends for multi-year demand validation
- Checked global search volume for dog products
- Defined niche: Dog owners who value personalization
- Set up Facebook Business Manager and Pixel
- Created brand identity (colors, logo, messaging)

**Launch Results:**

- Initial testing phase: $100 spent per product
- Tested 3 products weekly with 4 ad sets each
- Early conversion rates: 2-5% (high-performing campaigns hit 11%)

### Growth Journey

#### Phase 1: Testing & Validation (Months 1-3)

**What happened:**

- Started Facebook/Instagram ad campaigns
- Daily budget: **$3 per ad set** (~$84/month)
- Testing approach: $100 per product
- Tested 3 products/week with 4 ad sets each
- Focused on **conversion objective** (not awareness)

**Key tactics:**

**Revenue:** Scattered sales, break-even around $1,000 invested

**Marketing spend:** ~$3/day per ad set = ~$250-300/month estimated

**Lessons learned:**

- "Test out the audiences that you are displaying your ads to" (not just creative)
- Interest-based targeting (pet store shoppers) > demographic targeting
- Mobile placements convert better for impulse pet products

#### Phase 2: Stabilization & Scaling (Months 4-6)

**What happened:**

- Achieved **$60,000 monthly revenue** consistently
- Implemented abandoned cart recovery (email + SMS + messenger + retargeting)
- Launched email marketing (2-3 emails/week)
- Built Loox photo review system for social proof

**Key tactics:**

**Abandoned Cart Recovery:**

- Email campaigns (automated)
- SMS messages via TxtCart
- WhatsApp and Facebook Messenger via Recart
- Retargeting Facebook ads

**Email Marketing:**

- Mix of value content + product promotions
- Focused on dog care tips and personalization options

**Revenue:** $60,000/month sustained

#### Phase 3: Peak Performance (Oct-Dec Period)

**What happened:**

- Average monthly: ~$59,333
- Maintained **$60,000/month** peak performance

- Optimized Facebook ad audiences based on 4-6 month data

**Results:**

### Key Decisions

1. **Choosing Dog Niche (Before Launch)**
   - **Why:** Genuine passion + knowledge about dogs, validated by Google Trends
   - **Alternative considered:** Trending products, generic niches
   - **Outcome:** Passion enabled better product curation and customer understanding

2. **Spocket over AliExpress (Week 1)**
   - **Why:** AliExpress had "poor descriptions, generic quality, uncertain shipping"
   - **Alternative considered:** AliExpress (cheaper but worse quality)
   - **Outcome:** Better margins, faster shipping, higher customer satisfaction, fewer refunds

3. **Focusing on Personalization (MVP Phase)**
   - **Why:** Market gap in custom dog tags and bracelets
   - **Alternative considered:** Generic pet accessories
   - **Outcome:** Higher perceived value, better margins, defensible positioning

4. **$3/day per ad set (Testing Phase)**
   - **Why:** Controlled spending while validating products
   - **Alternative considered:** Higher daily budgets or no testing
   - **Outcome:** $100/product testing budget allowed systematic validation without overspending

5. **Multi-Channel Abandoned Cart Recovery (Month 4-6)**
   - **Why:** High cart abandonment rate in e-commerce (especially pet products)
   - **Channels:** Email + SMS + WhatsApp + Messenger + Retargeting ads
   - **Outcome:** Significantly increased conversion rate, recovered revenue

6. **Stripe via International Business Registration (Month 3-6)**
   - **Why:** Better payment processing, fewer restrictions than PayPal/2Checkout
   - **Alternative considered:** Staying with PayPal/2Checkout
   - **Outcome:** Reduced payment processing issues, better customer experience

7. **Testing Audiences over Creative (Ongoing)**
   - **Why:** Marc's philosophy: "Test out the audiences that you are displaying your ads to"
   - **Alternative considered:** Constantly changing ad creative
   - **Outcome:** Better ROAS, identified PetSmart/Petco customers as ideal audience

### Challenges & Solutions

1. **Challenge:** Supplier reliability and quality concerns
   - **Solution:**
     - Extensively vetted Spocket suppliers (not generic AliExpress)
     - Checked supplier ratings, reviews, shipping times
     - Ordered samples before listing products
     - Prioritized US/EU suppliers for quality and speed
   - **Result:** Better product quality, faster shipping, fewer refunds, higher customer satisfaction

2. **Challenge:** Product-market fit validation
   - **Solution:**
     - Systematic testing: $100 per product
     - Tested 3 products/week with 4 ad sets each
     - Discontinued underperforming products despite personal preference
     - Focused on data over gut feeling
   - **Result:** Only scaled winners, avoided wasting budget on losers

3. **Challenge:** Payment processing restrictions
   - **Solution:**
     - Started with PayPal and 2Checkout
     - Later established international business for Stripe access
     - Navigated jurisdiction requirements
   - **Result:** Better payment processing, reduced friction, improved conversion

4. **Challenge:** Cart abandonment (high rate in e-commerce)
   - **Solution:**
     - Multi-channel recovery: Email + SMS + WhatsApp + Messenger + Retargeting
     - Strategic pricing: Temporary discounts for hesitant buyers
     - After conversion, negotiated supplier discounts to maintain margins
     - Aggressive but non-intrusive approach
   - **Result:** Significantly increased conversion rate, recovered abandoned revenue

5. **Challenge:** Store design and performance
   - **Solution:**
     - Used Brooklyn theme (proven converter)
     - Balanced professional appearance with fast load times
     - "Your site needs to load within 3 seconds" philosophy
     - HD images but optimized file sizes
     - Sticky navigation for mobile
   - **Result:** Fast-loading store, better mobile experience, higher conversion

6. **Challenge:** Scaling Facebook ads profitably
   - **Solution:**
     - Started with $3/day per ad set (controlled testing)
     - Tested audiences > testing creative
     - Focused on interest-based targeting (PetSmart, Petco customers)
     - Scaled winning audiences incrementally
     - Maintained 2-5% conversion rate (11% on best campaigns)
   - **Result:** Sustainable $60K/month revenue from ads

7. **Challenge:** Balancing passion with profitability
   - **Solution:**
     - Chose dog niche due to genuine passion BUT validated with data
     - Discontinued products that didn't sell despite personal preference
     - "If you have a bad product, not even the most gorgeous looking store can sell it"
     - Data-driven decisions overrode emotional attachment
   - **Result:** Profitable business aligned with personal interest
