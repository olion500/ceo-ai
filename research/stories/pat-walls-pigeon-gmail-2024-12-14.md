# Pat Walls - Pigeon for Gmail Success Story


## The "Built for Myself" Story

### Personal Problem

**Pat's Gmail Frustration:**
> "Pat built Pigeon to solve his own needs and spend less time inside Gmail"

**Specific Pain Points:**
- Too much time spent in Gmail
- Mundane email tasks were repetitive
- Email follow-ups needed automation
- Needed CRM functionality without leaving Gmail
- Wanted to scale email management

**The Insight:**
As entrepreneur running Starter Story, Pat needed better email management but didn't want to leave Gmail for separate CRM tools.

## What Pigeon Does

**Product Description:**
> "Pigeon is a powerful CRM and automation suite for Gmail"

**Form Factor:**
- Chrome extension built on top of Gmail
- Works inside Gmail interface
- No separate app to learn

**Key Features:**
- Email automation
- Automated follow-ups
- CRM functionality
- Scales with user needs
- Streamlines mundane tasks

## The Journey to $1,000 MRR

### The Challenge

> "$1,000 MRR! (harder than I anticipated)"

**What Pat Learned:**
- Building SaaS was "a new type of challenge"
- Much harder than expected
- Different from building Starter Story (content business)

### Getting to 40 Paying Customers

**Timeline:**
- Described as "slowly reached 40 paying customers"
- Each customer represented learning experience
- ~$25 average revenue per customer ($1K MRR ÷ 40 customers)

### The Struggles

**Sales Calls:**
> "The initial sales calls were extremely time consuming and often led to no sale"

**Challenges:**
- ❌ Time-intensive sales process
- ❌ Low conversion rate from calls
- ❌ Harder than anticipated overall
- ❌ New type of business (SaaS vs content)

### What He Learned

**Despite struggles:**
- ✅ Learned about direct sales
- ✅ Understood customers deeply
- ✅ Learned about the market
- ✅ Made personal relationships with customers
- ✅ Validated problem-solution fit

## Solving Own Problem → Product

### The Classic Indie Hacker Path

2. **Built Solution:** Pigeon for Gmail
3. **Validated:** If Pat needed it, others do too

## Key Takeaways

### For Indie Hackers

1. **Different Products = Different Challenges**
   - Content business ≠ SaaS business
   - Each requires different skill sets
   - Be prepared for new learning curves

2. **Sales is Hard but Educational**
   - Time-consuming with low conversion
   - But builds deep customer understanding
   - Personal relationships add value beyond revenue

3. **$1K MRR is Real Achievement**
   - Not easy or quick
   - Represents 40+ customer conversations
   - Validates real problem-solution fit

4. **Build for Yourself Works**
   - Pat's own email pain = product opportunity
   - Dogfooding ensures product quality
   - Authentic understanding of customer needs
