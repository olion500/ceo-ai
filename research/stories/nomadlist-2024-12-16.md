# Nomad List Success Story - Pieter Levels


## The Story

### Problem Discovery

**Personal Context:** Pieter Levels sold his possessions to travel the world as a digital nomad. While traveling and working from different places, he met other remote workers—freelancers, remote employees, startup founders—doing the same thing.

**Key Problem Identified:**
- He knew some cities had entire digital nomad communities (like Chiang Mai)
- But it was hard to figure out what OTHER cities would be suitable for remote work
- No central index existed for remote workers to discover ideal cities
- Information was scattered, inconsistent, and hard to compare

**Insight:** "They needed a city index for remote workers"

**Timing Context:** Pieter was back in Amsterdam, living with his parents, and "starting to run out of money." He needed to build a profitable solo startup.

### MVP Development

**Timeline:** January 2014 (7th startup in "12 startups in 12 months" challenge)

**Inspiration:** Jennifer Dewalt's "180 websites in 180 days" project inspired his 12-month challenge

**Initial Features:**
- NOT a product initially - started as a **crowdsourced spreadsheet**
- Compiled city data: cost of living, internet speed, safety
- Shared publicly on Twitter with accidentally open edit permissions

**The "Accidental" Viral Launch:**
- Shared spreadsheet on Twitter
- Mistakenly set permissions to allow anyone to edit
- This "mistake" created huge buzz as strangers added cities and shared it
- Community crowdsourced the entire dataset organically

**Building the Product (Less than 1 month):**
- After viral spreadsheet success, built actual website in under a month
- Converted spreadsheet data into sortable, filterable website
- Focused on speed and simplicity over feature completeness

**Tech Stack (initial):**
- Simple web application
- Later: Extensive automation with 700-2,000 cron jobs
- No specific framework mentioned, focused on getting it working fast

**Development Philosophy:**
- "Launch quickly to see if it would get traction, reducing uncertainty"
- Treat projects "like cattle, not pets" - remain emotionally detached
- PURE problem-solving: Painful, Urgent, Recognized, Easy (for founder to solve)

**Costs:**
- Minimal (living with parents, bootstrapping from savings)
- No external investment

### Launch Strategy

**Platform:** Product Hunt + Hacker News

**Launch Date:** January 2014

**Launch Results:**
- Achieved #1 on BOTH Product Hunt AND Hacker News simultaneously
- Described as "a crazy two weeks"
- Exceeded performance of any prior project
- "Amazing and also stressful"

**Why It Worked:**
- Solved real problem for rapidly growing community (digital nomads)
- Simple, useful tool with immediate value
- Already had community validation from viral spreadsheet
- Perfect timing (remote work trend accelerating)

### Growth Journey

#### Phase 1: Viral Launch & Initial Growth (2014)
**Revenue: $0 → Initial paid memberships**

**Key Activities:**
- Launched on Product Hunt/HN with massive success
- Converted spreadsheet users to website users
- Started with completely free access
- Introduced paid membership to combat spam ($5 initial)

**Monetization Evolution:**
- Started free to build user base
- $5 one-time payment (to reduce spammers)
- $25 one-time payment (people kept joining)
- $50 one-time payment (continued raising as demand stayed strong)
- $65 one-time payment

**Community Building:**
- Initially used Slack for community
- Later built native platform features
- Created automated meetup system in popular nomad cities

#### Phase 2: Building the Ecosystem (2014-2019)
**Revenue: Growing MRR → $20K-$40K/month**

**Product Expansion:**
- Portfolio approach: Launched 8 projects total in 2014, focused on highest traction

**Nomad List Feature Evolution:**
- Trip logging functionality
- Climate-based location matching
- Dating features for nomads
- Community meetup automation
- City data APIs
- Tinder integration

**Operational Scaling:**
- Deployed 700-2,000 automated "robots" (cron jobs) depending on server load
- Automated: city data collection via APIs, trending city identification, meetup creation
- Quote: "Hiring increases complexity...Robots can be simple and very fast"
- Remained solo founder, rejected VC funding and hiring

#### Phase 3: Mature Business (2019 - Present)
**Revenue: $700K ARR (Nomad List), $3M ARR (total ecosystem)**

**2019 Milestone:** Launched Nomad List 5.0 after five years
- "Used by millions of people every month"
- Thousands of paying members
- Stable subscription revenue model

**Current State:**
- $20,000-$40,000 monthly revenue (Nomad List alone, ~$300K-$480K annually)
- Additional revenue from Remote OK, Rebase, Interior AI
- **Total ecosystem: $3M ARR**
- Still solo founder with no employees
- Completely bootstrapped, no VC

**Philosophy:**
- Positioned within mission on "the future of remote work"
- Prediction: "1 billion digital nomads by 2035"
- Remote workers will become location-independent globally

### Key Decisions

1. **12 Startups in 12 Months Challenge (2014)**
   - **Why:** Running out of money, needed to find something that worked
   - **Result:** Nomad List was #7, became most successful
   - **Lesson:** Rapid iteration reveals what works; don't over-invest in unproven ideas

2. **Starting with Spreadsheet Instead of Product (Jan 2014)**
   - **Why:** Faster to validate demand, crowdsource data
   - **Context:** "Accidental" open edit permissions created viral sharing
   - **Result:** Massive organic validation before building anything
   - **Lesson:** Sometimes the simplest MVP is not even a product

3. **Building in Under 1 Month (Early 2014)**
   - **Why:** Already had validation from spreadsheet, wanted to capitalize on momentum
   - **Result:** Fast launch while attention was high, captured early adopters
   - **Lesson:** Speed matters when you have validation

4. **Progressive Price Increases ($5 → $65)**
   - **Why:** Testing willingness to pay, reducing spam, maximizing revenue
   - **Context:** Started at $5 to combat spammers, kept raising as demand stayed strong
   - **Result:** Found optimal price point without losing customers
   - **Lesson:** Don't be afraid to raise prices if value is there

5. **Shift to Annual Recurring (April, year unspecified)**
   - **Why:** Predictable revenue, better cash flow, customer commitment
   - **Result:** Sign-ups remained consistent, revenue became more stable
   - **Lesson:** Subscription model works for community platforms

6. **Staying Solo / No Hiring**
   - **Why:** "Hiring increases complexity"
   - **Context:** Chose automation (700-2,000 cron jobs) over employees
   - **Result:** Higher margins, full control, no management overhead
   - **Lesson:** Automation can replace hiring for many tasks

7. **No VC Funding**
   - **Why:** Maintain control, keep equity, avoid growth pressure
   - **Result:** Built sustainably, profitable from early on, complete autonomy
   - **Lesson:** Bootstrapping works for capital-efficient businesses

8. **Building Portfolio of Related Products**
   - **Why:** Leverage existing audience, create synergies, diversify revenue
   - **Context:** Remote OK, Rebase, Hoodmaps, Interior AI all target similar users
   - **Result:** $3M ARR ecosystem vs single product reliance
   - **Lesson:** Portfolio approach reduces risk, maximizes audience value

9. **Engineering as Marketing**
   - **Why:** Build useful tools that naturally promote main products
   - **Context:** Hoodmaps, city APIs, Tinder integration
   - **Result:** Organic marketing through utility, no paid ads
   - **Lesson:** Free tools can be powerful marketing

10. **Treating Projects "Like Cattle, Not Pets"**
    - **Why:** Emotional detachment enables ruthless focus on what works
    - **Context:** Launched 8 projects in 2014, only focused on top performers
    - **Result:** Didn't waste time on losers, doubled down on winners
    - **Lesson:** Kill projects quickly if they don't work
