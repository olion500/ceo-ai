# CruiseCup & Solefully Success Story


## The Story

### Problem Discovery

**Initial Discovery (Age 14-15):**
Michael Satterlee learned 3D printing in his freshman year Design and Drawing for Production (DDP) class at Shenendehowa High School in Clifton Park, New York. He learned computer-aided design (CAD) and 3D printing fundamentals through this class.

**First Business - Sand Repellent Company:**
Before both Solefully and CruiseCup, Satterlee started a sand repellent company (details not fully documented). This first venture provided funding for Solefully.

**Solefully Discovery (Age 16):**

- Saw a viral story about someone selling snowplow attachments for Crocs
- Recognized market gap: Crocs accessories were trending but not oversaturated
- Started experimenting with various products on Etsy in 2023
- Snowplow attachment for clogs took off
- Decided to "build an entire store around that niche"

Key insight: "I focused on finding a product that was simple to produce, easy to market and not already oversaturated."

**CruiseCup Discovery (Age 17-18):**

- Built on lessons learned from Solefully
- Created "tactical reload can holder" - a 3D printed beverage holder that automatically ejects used cans when new ones are inserted
- Designed for energy drinks and other canned beverages
- Novel concept that viewers "hadn't seen anything like it before"

### MVP Development

**Solefully MVP:**

**Timeline:** Started sophomore year (2023) with one 3D printer

**Initial Investment:**

- $300 cash
- 1 3D printer (approximately $100-200 based on later quotes)
- Filament rolls (~$20 each)

**Initial Products:**

- Snowplow attachment (hero product)
- Vape holders that attach to side of shoe
- Toe charms
- Various Crocs accessories

**Tech Stack:**

- Hardware: Desktop 3D printers (consumer-grade)
- Software: CAD modeling software (easy to use, AI-assisted options available)
- Filament: Standard 3D printing filament (~$20/roll)
- Platform: Etsy (initially), then own website

**Development Process:**

1. Learned CAD in DDP class
2. Experimented with various product ideas on Etsy
3. Tested market response through social media content
4. Identified winning product (snowplow attachment)
5. Built dedicated store around Crocs accessories niche
6. Scaled to 50+ printers within ~1 year

**CruiseCup MVP:**

**Timeline:** Launched 2024-2025 after Solefully success

**Initial Features:**

- Tactical reload can holder (signature product)
- Automatic can ejection mechanism
- Compatible with energy drink cans
- Various designs and colors

**Development Philosophy:**
> "Just make whatever idea you have exist first, and then make it good later"

Rather than spending months perfecting, he created quickly and iterated based on customer feedback.

### Key Decisions

1. **Decision: Move from Etsy experiments to dedicated Crocs accessories store (Solefully)**
   - **Why:** Snowplow attachment showed strong demand; recognized opportunity to own a niche rather than sell random products
   - **Reasoning:** "I focused on finding a product that was simple to produce, easy to market and not already oversaturated"
   - **Result:** Built focused brand with consistent product line, easier marketing, clearer value proposition

2. **Decision: Post 3 TikToks/Instagram Reels daily, never run paid ads**
   - **Why:** Believed in organic content-driven growth; young entrepreneur advantage in spotting trends
   - **Reasoning:** "You're spotting trends subconsciously that these big brands, they're paying people hundreds of thousand dollars to spot. That's why I think being young is such a weapon."
   - **Result:** 50M monthly impressions organically, zero ad spend, authentic community building

3. **Decision: Move to warehouse space (Solefully)**
   - **Why:** Home operations became chaotic and inefficient; printers everywhere, materials throughout house
   - **Context:** "The business completely took over my entire house. There were printers in my bedroom, boxes in the hallway and packaging materials in the kitchen."
   - **Result:** Massive improvement in efficiency and focus; could scale operations properly; better work-life separation

4. **Decision: Keep production in-house vs outsourcing to injection molding or overseas manufacturing**
   - **Why:** Maintains design flexibility, rapid iteration capability, and personal control
   - **Reasoning:** Avoids extensive tooling costs, enables quick product launches, can respond to trends immediately
   - **Result:** Operational flexibility traditional manufacturing cannot match; can launch new designs within days; no minimum order quantities

5. **Decision: Share behind-the-scenes content showing print farm, packing, shipping, business insights**
   - **Why:** Build trust and sustain momentum post-virality
   - **Context:** Authenticity-driven approach to content creation
   - **Result:** Community engagement, customer trust, sustained interest beyond viral moments, continuous ideation from audience

6. **Decision: Launch CruiseCup after Solefully success rather than just scaling Solefully**
   - **Why:** Recognized opportunity to apply learnings to new product category; diversify revenue streams
   - **Result:** Achieved even greater success with CruiseCup ($300K/month vs $20K/month); validated content-first approach works across products

7. **Decision: Buy printers in batches of 30 during viral surge**
   - **Why:** Demand far exceeded capacity; needed to fulfill orders
   - **Context:** "When Cruise Cup hit viral status, I'd be buying batches of 30 printers and still not being able to keep up"
   - **Result:** Able to meet demand; scaled to 130+ printers; maintained customer satisfaction during explosive growth

8. **Decision: "Just make whatever idea you have exist first, and then make it good later"**
   - **Why:** 3D printing allows rapid, low-cost prototyping; test market before perfecting
   - **Reasoning:** "That's a major advantage of working with a 3D printer: you can create products within days and at a low cost, and then test the market before deciding to invest time, money, and materials in producing something at scale"
   - **Result:** Faster time to market, lower risk, data-driven product development

### Founder's Mindset & Decision-Making

**Initial Beliefs:**

  Initially might have believed in perfecting products before showing them; shifted to "Just make whatever idea you have exist first, and then make it good later"

  Proved you can start with just $300 and 1 printer; "You could get a 3D printer for like 100 bucks, and a roll of filament costs like $20"

  Could have seen youth as disadvantage; instead recognized "being young is such a weapon" for trend-spotting

**Changed Beliefs:**

  - Initial: Make product, then market it
  - Changed to: "Start by posting, not just making" - validate through content first

  - Could have assumed paid ads necessary for e-commerce
  - Proved 100% organic content can generate 50M monthly impressions and $300K/month revenue

  - Might have considered outsourcing to China or injection molding as "proper" scaling
  - Learned in-house 3D printing provides "operational flexibility that traditional manufacturing cannot match"

  - Initially thought home was fine
  - Learned dedicated warehouse "significantly improved efficiency and focus" - should have moved sooner

  - Learned from viral surge crisis: need better systems before viral moments, not after

**Critical Decisions Explained:**

1. **Decision: Build entire store around Crocs accessories niche**
   - **Context:** Experimenting with various random products on Etsy; snowplow attachment started selling well
   - **Options Considered:**
     - Continue selling random 3D printed items
     - Focus on one winning product only
     - Build dedicated niche store around product category
   - **Why Chose This:** "I focused on finding a product that was simple to produce, easy to market and not already oversaturated" - niche focus provides clarity
   - **Outcome:** Solefully brand identity, $250K annual revenue, sustainable $20K/month

2. **Decision: Post 3 times daily on TikTok/Instagram, never run ads**
   - **Context:** Young entrepreneur with zero audience, limited capital
   - **Options Considered:**
     - Paid advertising campaigns
     - Occasional organic posting
     - Daily consistent organic content
   - **Why Chose This:** "You're spotting trends subconsciously that these big brands, they're paying people hundreds of thousand dollars to spot" - youth as competitive advantage; free platforms reduce risk
   - **Outcome:** 50M monthly impressions organically, zero ad spend, $300K revenue months

3. **Decision: Manage 130+ printers in-house vs outsource manufacturing**
   - **Context:** CruiseCup going viral, demand exploding
   - **Options Considered:**
     - Outsource to injection molding manufacturer
     - Partner with Chinese manufacturers
     - Buy more printers and manage in-house
   - **Why Chose This:** Rapid iteration capability, no minimum orders, design flexibility, immediate market response
   - **Outcome:** "Operational flexibility that traditional manufacturing cannot match"; can launch new designs in days; higher margins

4. **Decision: Launch CruiseCup rather than just scaling Solefully**
   - **Context:** Solefully successful at $20K/month stable; could just focus on growing it
   - **Options Considered:**
     - Focus 100% on scaling Solefully
     - Launch new product line within Solefully
     - Create entirely new brand (CruiseCup)
   - **Why Chose This:** Apply proven content-first model to new category; diversify revenue; test if model works beyond Crocs niche
   - **Outcome:** Validated model works across products; achieved 15x revenue with CruiseCup vs Solefully; reduced single-product dependency

5. **Decision: Move to warehouse from home**
   - **Context:** "The business completely took over my entire house. There were printers in my bedroom, boxes in the hallway and packaging materials in the kitchen"
   - **Options Considered:**
     - Continue home operations
     - Rent small office space
     - Get dedicated warehouse
   - **Why Chose This:** Chaos impacting efficiency and home life; needed room for 50+ printers and inventory
   - **Outcome:** "Significantly improved efficiency and focus" - major breakthrough; able to scale to 130+ printers later
