# Tim Kock Watch Store Dropshipping Success Story


## The Story

### Problem Discovery

Tim Kock wanted to prove that **dropshipping is achievable for ordinary people** while maintaining a full-time job. His motivation was to:

- Document the entire process transparently
- Show that entrepreneurship doesn't require special talents
- Build a business while working full-time
- Test if determination alone could lead to success

**Key insight:** "I wanted to document my entire process of building, launching, and running a dropshipping business" - demonstrating that side hustles are feasible for regular employees.

### MVP Development

**Timeline:**

**Initial Features:**

- Basic Shopify store with core pages (contact, about, policies)
- Payment gateways: PayPal Express and Stripe
- Free shipping model to all countries
- 10 product variants (watches in different colors/styles)
- Simple product descriptions (1-3 lines each)
- Mobile-optimized theme

**Tech Stack:**

**Development Process:**

- Kept store design minimal and fast-loading ("Your site needs to load within 3 seconds")
- Used free or low-cost tools to minimize upfront investment
- Focused on curated product selection (10 products vs. hundreds)
- After pineapple store failure, pivoted to watches in 40 minutes by applying lessons learned

**Platform:** Instagram Direct Messages (primary), Facebook Ads (tested)
**Date:** Not explicitly stated (2017 approximate)

### Growth Journey

#### Phase 1: Failed Pineapple Store (Weeks 1-8)

**What happened:**

- Selected pineapple-themed products (bracelets, hats, necklaces, earrings, dresses)
- Spent $66.40 on Facebook ads → 1 add-to-cart, 0 sales
- Partnered with 10 micro-influencers (5K+ followers) → gave free products (~$40 cost) → 612 visitors, 0 sales

**Key learnings:**

- Facebook ads failed to convert for low-ticket items
- Free influencer partnerships didn't drive sales for unvalidated products
- Generic niche with no clear target audience
- Documented all mistakes before pivoting

**Tactic used:**

- Listed all failures and created new S.M.A.R.T. goal within 40 minutes of deciding to pivot

#### Phase 2: Watch Store - Finding Traction (Weeks 1-3)

**What happened:**

- Built new store in under 30 minutes using lessons learned
- Defined specific target: European women aged 20-29 interested in fitness/travel/lifestyle
- Started Instagram DM outreach on day 1

**Key tactic - Instagram Direct Messages:**

- Sent 12 DMs in week 1-2
- Personalized messages to potential customers
- Result: **7 sales ($166.99)**

**Revenue:** $166.99 (7 orders)
**Marketing spend:** $0 (organic DMs only)

#### Phase 3: Scaling (Weeks 3-5)

**What happened:**

- Scaled Instagram DM strategy to 60 messages per day
- Segmented by hashtags: 20 DMs to #fitness, 20 to #matcha, 20 to #travel
- Launched affiliate program with existing customers (10% commission)
- Recruited 5 brand ambassadors from buyer pool

**Results:**

- Week 4-5: 54 orders totaling $1,783.87
- 24 orders ($672.49) came from DM strategy in first 16 days of scaling

**Revenue (Weeks 3-5):** $1,783.87 (54 orders)
**Marketing tactics:** DMs + affiliate program (still mostly organic)

#### Phase 4: Micro-Influencer Partnerships (Weeks 6-8)

**What happened:**

- Partnered with 10 micro-influencers (5,000+ followers)
- Cost: $50 products + $5 express shipping per influencer = $55 each
- Total influencer investment: ~$550
- Result: Significant sales spike

**Week 8 results:**

   - **How:** Leveraged earlier research on steel strap watches showing validated demand
   - **Result:** First sales within 2-3 weeks vs. 0 sales in 8 weeks with pineapple

2. **Choosing Instagram DMs over Facebook Ads**
   - **Why:** Facebook ads failed spectacularly ($66.40 spent, 0 sales)
   - **How:** Manually sent personalized DMs to hashtag followers
   - **Result:** 7 sales immediately from 12 DMs (58% response rate implied)

3. **Setting S.M.A.R.T. Goal: $5,000 in 5 weeks**
   - **Why:** Needed clear, measurable target to stay focused
   - **Goal:** "Make $5,000 in sales with a dropshipping business I built from scratch in 5 weeks" ($1,000/week target)
   - **Result:** Achieved $6,666.73 in 54 days (slightly over timeline but exceeded revenue goal)

4. **Limiting to 10 Products**
   - **Why:** "Clarity and manageability" - easier to test and optimize
   - **How:** Curated 10 watch variants based on earlier validation
   - **Result:** Streamlined store allowed faster decisions and better focus

5. **Creating Affiliate Program Early**
   - **Why:** Turn customers into evangelists with 10% commission incentive
   - **How:** Used Affiliatly app, recruited 5 early buyers as brand ambassadors
   - **Result:** Extended reach without paid ads

### Pivots & Changes

  - Changed niche completely based on data (validated watch demand)
  - Switched target audience from generic to specific (European women 20-29, fitness/travel)
  - Changed marketing from Facebook ads to Instagram DMs

  - Facebook: $66.40 spent, 1 cart, 0 sales
  - Instagram DMs: $0 spent, 7+ sales immediately

  - First store: Spent weeks building "perfect" store
  - Second store: Built functional store in 30 minutes
  - Lesson: "Don't overcomplicate it! Take action"

### Challenges & Solutions

1. **Challenge:** First store (pineapple) generated 0 sales despite traffic
   - **Solution:**
     - Documented all mistakes in detail
     - Pivoted to validated niche (watches) within 40 minutes
     - Set new S.M.A.R.T. goal ($5K in 5 weeks)
   - **Result:** 7 sales within 2-3 weeks of relaunch

2. **Challenge:** Long shipping times (2+ weeks from suppliers)
   - **Solution:**
     - Transparent communication about shipping expectations
     - Express shipping for influencer samples ($5 extra)
   - **Result:** Still resulted in some refunds, but managed expectations reduced complaints

3. **Challenge:** Instagram spam detection risk
   - **Solution:**
     - Limited DMs to avoid account ban
     - Personalized every message
     - Segmented by hashtags (20 DMs each: fitness, matcha, travel)
   - **Result:** Avoided spam filters, maintained account health

4. **Challenge:** Inconsistent influencer performance
   - **Solution:**
     - Vetted influencers for engagement rate (not just follower count)
     - Only worked with accounts showing 5K+ followers + activity
   - **Result:** More predictable results in second attempt vs. pineapple store

5. **Challenge:** High customer support burden
   - **Solution:**
     - Set clear shipping policies on website
     - Automated responses for common questions
     - Used Google Forms and Instagram polls for FAQs
   - **Result:** Reduced inquiry volume

6. **Challenge:** Balancing full-time job with dropshipping
   - **Solution:**
     - Worked part-time on business (~3 months investment)
     - Used Asana for task management
     - Automated fulfillment via Oberlo
   - **Result:** Successfully ran business alongside employment

   - **Why Chose This:** Avoid analysis paralysis, test if product matters more than store design
   - **Outcome:** Store generated sales immediately, validated speed > perfection

### Marketing Channel Breakdown

**Channel 1: Instagram Direct Messages**

  - Hashtag research: #gym, #travelblogger, #matchalover
  - Target identification: Women 20-29, European, fitness/travel interested
  - Personalized messaging (not copy-paste spam)
  - Scaling: 12 DMs → 60 DMs/day (20 per hashtag)
  - Segmentation testing: 20 fitness, 20 matcha, 20 travel
  - Week 1-2: 7 sales from 12 DMs
  - Week 3+: 24 orders ($672.49) in 16 days
  - Became primary customer acquisition channel
  - Infinite ROI (free vs. $66.40 Facebook failure)
  - Personal relationship-building > paid ads for early validation
  - Limit to 60/day to avoid Instagram spam detection
  - Segment by hashtag to test audience fit
  - Only works with validated product (failed with pineapple)

**Channel 2: Micro-Influencer Partnerships (5K+ followers)**

  - Identified 10 micro-influencers with 5,000+ followers
  - Verified engagement rates (not just follower count)
  - Sent free products ($50 value) + express shipping ($5)
  - Asked for authentic reviews/posts
  - Tracked performance by influencer
  - Initial outreach: 4 hours (research + messaging)
  - Follow-up: 1-2 hours total
  - Minimal ongoing management
  - Significant sales spike in weeks 6-8
  - Contributed to 125 total orders by week 8
  - Social proof and traffic boost
  - **Compare to pineapple:** Same tactic, 612 visitors, 0 sales (product issue, not channel issue)
  - Only works after product validation (not for untested products)
  - Engagement rate > follower count
  - Express shipping ($5) worth the cost for faster results
  - Vetted influencers perform better

**Channel 3: Affiliate Program**

  - Installed Affiliatly Shopify app
  - Set 10% commission structure
  - Recruited 5 existing customers as brand ambassadors
  - Provided unique referral links
  - Tracked sales and paid commissions
  - 1 hour setup
  - 30 minutes/week management
  - Minimal ongoing effort
  - Affiliatly app subscription (not disclosed)
  - 10% commission on referred sales only (ROI-positive)
  - 5 active brand ambassadors
  - Extended reach through word-of-mouth
  - Ongoing passive acquisition channel
  - Turn satisfied customers into salespeople
  - Commission-only model = no risk
  - Launch after product validation, not before

**Channel 4: Facebook Ads (FAILED)**

  - Conversion campaign objective
  - Generic pineapple lover targeting
  - Mobile feed placements
  - 1 add-to-cart (never converted)
  - 0 sales
  - CAC: $66.40 per cart (infinite per sale)
  - Don't use paid ads for unvalidated products
  - Low-ticket items + cold traffic = poor ROI
  - Organic validation (DMs) before paid scaling
  - Wrong product > wrong channel

**Channel 5: Free Influencer Partnerships (FAILED - Pineapple)**

  - 10 micro-influencers with 5K+ followers
  - Free products (~$40 cost total)
  - 612 unique visitors
  - 0 sales
  - Conversion rate: 0%
  - Traffic ≠ Sales without product-market fit
  - "If you have a bad product, not even the most gorgeous looking store can sell it"
  - Same tactic worked with watches (product difference, not channel)
